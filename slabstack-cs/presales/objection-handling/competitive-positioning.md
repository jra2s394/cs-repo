# Competitive Positioning

How Slabstack compares to the alternatives prospects are usually evaluating. Keep responses factual and grounded — do not make claims about competitors that you can't substantiate.

---

## Position Statement

Slabstack is the customer-facing operations layer for ready-mix and concrete producers. It sits between your dispatch/ERP system and your customer, replacing the spreadsheet-and-email workflow most producers use today for quotes, project tracking, and customer communication.

---

## Common Alternatives

### Spreadsheets + Email

**Their pitch:** "It's free and we know how to use it."

**What's true:**
- It is free up front
- Everyone knows how to use it
- It works fine for low volume

**What breaks at scale:**
- No single source of truth — quotes live in one place, project status in another, customer communication in inboxes
- Version control issues — three people editing the same quote spreadsheet
- Nothing carries forward — every job rebuilds from scratch
- No customer-facing view

**How to position Slabstack:** Don't attack the spreadsheet. Quantify the hidden cost: how many hours per week does the team spend reconciling between sheet and inbox? At any meaningful volume, the math wins on its own.

---

### Generic CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.)

**Their pitch:** "We can configure a CRM to do this."

**What's true:**
- A general CRM can be configured to track customers and projects
- If the company already pays for one, marginal cost is low

**What breaks:**
- Generic CRMs don't speak concrete — no mix designs, no cubic yards, no dispatch integration
- Heavy configuration burden up front; ongoing maintenance to keep it working
- Customer-facing experience is generic — not designed for a ready-mix workflow

**How to position Slabstack:** "A CRM is a great fit for tracking sales pipeline. Slabstack is a great fit for what happens after the sale closes — turning a quote into a delivered job. Most of our customers use both."

---

### Dispatch System "Customer Portal" Add-Ons

**Their pitch:** "Our dispatch vendor has a customer portal module."

**What's true:**
- The integration depth with their existing dispatch system is unmatched
- It's a single vendor relationship

**What breaks:**
- Dispatch-system portals are usually built as a feature, not a product — limited investment in the customer experience
- Locked to that dispatch vendor; switching costs increase over time
- Often charged per-customer-seat, which scales badly

**How to position Slabstack:** "Slabstack is purpose-built for the customer experience side. We integrate with your dispatch system, so you keep the dispatch system you have — but the customer-facing workflow is built by a team focused only on that problem."

---

### Build It In-House

**Their pitch:** "We have a developer / we'll just build something."

**What's true:**
- A custom build can fit the exact workflow
- For very large producers, this can make sense

**What breaks:**
- Building is the easy part; maintaining for five years is where it falls apart
- One developer leaves and the institutional knowledge goes with them
- Opportunity cost — every dollar spent on internal tooling is a dollar not spent on the core business

**How to position Slabstack:** "If you have the team for it, in-house can absolutely work. The trade-off is who maintains it in three years. Slabstack is built by a team that does only this — you get the benefit of every customer's feedback compounding into the product."

---

## Rules for Competitive Conversations

1. **Never name a competitor first.** Let them bring it up.
2. **Don't trash competitors.** It makes us look insecure and gives the competitor free attention.
3. **Stick to public facts.** No claims about competitor pricing, customer counts, or roadmaps unless you can cite a source.
4. **Redirect to value.** "I can speak to what we do well. The best way to compare is to run both in a pilot and let your team decide."
5. **Log new competitive intel.** If a prospect mentions a competitor we don't have notes on, add it after the call.
