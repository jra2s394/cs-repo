# Demo Script

Default Slabstack demo, 30 minutes. Tailor every section to the pain points surfaced during discovery — do not run this script verbatim if discovery uncovered something specific.

---

## Pre-Demo Prep

- [ ] Review discovery notes
- [ ] Build a demo project that mirrors the prospect's workflow (similar customer name, similar job type, similar volume)
- [ ] Pre-load mix designs and prices that look like theirs
- [ ] Confirm attendees on the invite and their roles

---

## Agenda

| Section | Time | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome & re-confirm pain points | 3 min | CSM/AE |
| Quote-to-send walkthrough | 8 min | CSM |
| Project tracking | 8 min | CSM |
| Communication & customer view | 5 min | CSM |
| Integration with dispatch (if relevant) | 3 min | CSM |
| Q&A and next steps | 3 min | All |

---

## 1. Welcome & Re-Confirm Pain Points (3 min)

- Thank them for being back on
- Read back the top 2–3 pain points from discovery in their own words
- "Today I'm going to show how Slabstack handles those three things specifically. Stop me at any time."

---

## 2. Quote-to-Send Walkthrough (8 min)

- Start on the empty dashboard. Talk through what a Monday morning looks like for an estimator.
- Click **New Quote**. Pick a customer that resembles theirs.
- Add line items with their mix designs at their prices.
- Show the side-by-side preview as you type.
- Click **Send**. Show the email composer with the auto-attached PDF.
- Send it (to your own demo inbox), then show the email landing.

**Tie back:** "You mentioned that quotes today take 30+ minutes between the spreadsheet and the email. This was 90 seconds."

---

## 3. Project Tracking (8 min)

- Show the Projects list, filtered by Active.
- Open the project that came from the quote you just sent.
- Walk through the tabs — Overview, Quotes, Deliveries, Communication.
- Add a delivery, show the status update.
- Show a project that has been marked Complete and how it stays searchable.

**Tie back:** Reference the specific pain point about "where is my pour?" from discovery.

---

## 4. Communication & Customer View (5 min)

- Open the Communication tab on the same project.
- Show the email thread, internal notes, and how a customer reply lands here automatically.
- If their team has multiple people who touch one job, emphasize that anyone can pick up where the last person left off.

---

## 5. Integration with Dispatch (3 min) — *only if relevant*

- If they use Sysdyne (or another supported dispatch system), show how orders and deliveries flow in.
- Confirm scope: which data syncs, in which direction, how often.
- Be honest about what's not in scope.

---

## 6. Q&A and Next Steps (3 min)

- Pause: "What's resonating? What's missing?"
- Listen for objections — handle in the moment if small, log for follow-up if large.
- Confirm the next step: pricing conversation, security review, pilot, etc.
- Send a recap email same day.

---

## Common Pitfalls

- Don't demo features they didn't ask about. Stay tight to discovery.
- Don't apologize for things the product doesn't do yet — be direct: "Not today. On the roadmap."
- Don't end without a concrete next step on the calendar.
