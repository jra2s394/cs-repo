# Value Case

A one-pager the buyer can take internally to justify the purchase. Use after the ROI calculation is done.

---

## Audience

The buyer's boss, partner, accountant, or board — someone who wasn't on the demo and needs to be convinced in five minutes of reading.

---

## Structure

### 1. The Problem (1 paragraph)

Describe the current state in the buyer's own words. Use specifics from discovery — not generic pain.

**Bad:** "Operations are inefficient."
**Good:** "Today, every quote takes about 25 minutes between the spreadsheet, the price list, and the email. Across 120 quotes a month, that's 50 hours of estimator time spent on data entry."

---

### 2. The Cost (numbers)

Lift the totals from the ROI calculator. Three bullets max:

- Estimator time spent on quote/project admin today: **___ hours/month**
- Billing errors and rework: **$___ /year**
- Deals lost to slow follow-up: **$___ /year**

---

### 3. The Proposal

One paragraph on what Slabstack changes:

- What gets eliminated (specific workflows)
- What gets faster (specific tasks with before/after times)
- What becomes visible that isn't visible today

---

### 4. The Numbers

```
Annual investment in Slabstack:   $___
Annual value to the business:     $___
Net annual value:                 $___
Payback period:                   ___ months
```

---

### 5. Risk

Address head-on. Don't pretend there's none.

- **Switching cost:** ___ hours of setup, mostly in the first 30 days
- **Adoption risk:** ___ people need to learn it; onboarding videos and a CSM are included
- **Vendor risk:** Slabstack is purpose-built for ready-mix; if it doesn't work in the first 30 days, here's the cancellation path

---

### 6. The Ask

One sentence. What action do you want the reader to approve?

> "Approve the [tier] Slabstack subscription at $___/year for a 12-month commitment, starting [date]."

---

## Format Notes

- Keep this to one page. Two at the absolute most.
- No screenshots in the value case — those go in the demo.
- Send as a PDF, not a slide deck. PDFs forward.
- Match the customer's company colors or letterhead if you have them. It signals you took their decision seriously.
