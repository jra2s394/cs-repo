## 60-Day Check-In

**Target date:** ___________________
**Actual date:** ___________________

### Pre-meeting

- [ ] Compare 30-day usage to 60-day usage — trending up or down?
- [ ] Resolve any open items from 30-day recap
- [ ] Identify expansion signals (more users, additional plants, additional features)

### During meeting (30 min)

- [ ] Review usage trend
- [ ] Resolve 30-day open items
- [ ] Introduce one new workflow they haven't used yet
- [ ] Discuss any team changes on their side that affect adoption

### After meeting

- [ ] Send recap email with action items
- [ ] Update customer master `Health Score`
- [ ] If expansion signals present, flag in Asana for `/expansion` review
- [ ] Schedule 90-day check-in

**Health verdict at 60 days:** 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴
**Adoption trajectory:** Up / Flat / Down

## 90-Day Check-In

**Target date:** ___________________
**Actual date:** ___________________

### Pre-meeting

- [ ] Run `/qbr <name>` for a full quarter snapshot
- [ ] Pull ROI calculation if possible (time saved, errors prevented)
- [ ] Identify whether they should be moved off "onboarding" cadence to "active" cadence

### During meeting (45 min — this is the first mini-QBR)

- [ ] Walk through 90-day usage and value
- [ ] Confirm the customer would recommend Slabstack (NPS gut check)
- [ ] Identify next 90-day goals
- [ ] Confirm renewal timeline visibility (they should know the date)

### After meeting

- [ ] Send recap email
- [ ] Mark onboarding officially complete in Asana
- [ ] Move customer to active QBR cadence in customer master
- [ ] Move from weekly to monthly internal touchpoint
- [ ] If renewal is within 6 months, run `/renewal-health` to start prep

**Onboarding officially closed?** ☐ Yes   ☐ No (with reason)

---

## Red Flags Across All Three Checkpoints

Escalate to manager same day if:
- Usage drops more than 30% between checkpoints
- Customer cancels a check-in twice in a row
- Customer-side champion leaves the company
- More than 2 P0/P1 Shortcut stories open against the customer
- ARR at risk (signal of consolidation, budget cut, merger)
