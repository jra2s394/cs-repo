## Script

---

`[SHOW: Slabstack dashboard]`

Welcome back. In this video, we're covering Activities — one of the most useful habits you can build in Slabstack.

Activities are how you keep a record of everything that happens on your accounts: calls you've made, notes from site visits, emails you've sent, follow-ups you need to track. They live directly on your projects and customers, so nothing gets lost and your whole team stays on the same page.

Let's look at how it works.

---

### Part 1: Logging an Activity

`[CLICK: Projects in the left-hand navigation]`
`[CLICK: an existing project to open it]`
`[SHOW: Project detail page]`

Activities are logged at the record level — meaning you log them directly on a project or a customer. Open any project to follow along.

`[ZOOM: Activities tab or section within the project]`
`[CLICK: Activities tab]`
`[SHOW: Activities feed or list]`

Inside the project, click the **Activities** tab. This is the full history of everything that's been logged against this project — sorted by date, most recent at the top.

`[CLICK: Log Activity button or + Add Activity]`
`[SHOW: Activity creation form or modal]`

Click **Log Activity** to add a new one.

`[ZOOM: Activity Type dropdown]`

First, choose your activity type. You'll typically see options like **Call**, **Note**, **Email**, and **Meeting**. Pick whichever matches what happened.

`[CLICK: Activity Type — select "Call" as example]`
`[ZOOM: Notes / Description field]`
`[TYPE: brief example note — e.g., "Called to confirm pour schedule for Friday. Customer confirmed 7am start."]`

Add your notes in the description field. Keep it simple — just enough that anyone on your team can pick up where you left off.

`[ZOOM: Date field]`

Confirm the date, then save.

`[CLICK: Save]`
`[SHOW: Activity now visible in the feed]`

The activity appears in the feed immediately, timestamped and attached to the project.

---

### Part 2: Logging Activities on a Customer

`[CLICK: Customers in the left-hand navigation]`
`[CLICK: a customer record]`
`[SHOW: Customer detail page]`
`[CLICK: Activities tab]`

You can do the exact same thing on a customer record. Any activity logged here ties to the customer directly — useful for tracking relationship history across all their projects, not just one.

`[ZOOM: Activities tab showing existing log entries]`

Over time, this becomes a full picture of every interaction you've had with that account.

---

### Part 3: Viewing and Filtering Activities

`[CLICK: Activities in the left-hand navigation — if a standalone section exists]`
`[SHOW: Activities list view — all records]`

If you want a view across all your accounts at once, go to **Activities** in the left nav. This gives you everything in one place.

`[ZOOM: filter bar at the top]`

Use the filters to narrow by date range, activity type, or team member. If you need to see everything logged this week, or all calls from a specific rep, it's a few clicks.

---

`[SHOW: Slabstack dashboard or project view]`

That's Activities. It's a simple habit — log as you go — and it pays off fast when you're managing multiple accounts and need a clear paper trail.

In Video 4, we'll cover creating your first quote: how to build it, price it, and send it to a customer.

See you there.
