# Video 5: Managing Projects

**Series:** Slabstack Onboarding
**Runtime target:** 2:30–3:00
**Audience:** Brand new customers
**Tools:** Loom (recording) · Descript (editing) · ElevenLabs (voiceover)

---

## On-Screen Cues Legend

- `[SHOW]` — display this screen or element
- `[CLICK]` — click this item
- `[ZOOM]` — zoom or highlight this area
- `[TYPE]` — keyboard input visible on screen
- `[CUT TO]` — transition to next screen

---

## Flow

1. Navigate to Projects in the left nav
2. Click New Project
3. Select location
4. Add project name and key details (address, start date)
5. Add the buyer — pulls from configured defaults
6. Add other companies if applicable (GC, owner, etc.)
7. Add products (mixes) — pulls from configured defaults
8. Link an existing quote to the project (if applicable)
9. Update project status as work progresses (e.g., Active, On Hold, Closed)
10. Use the grid view to filter and manage all active projects at once

---

## Script

---

`[SHOW: Slabstack dashboard]`

Welcome back. You've got your account set up, your configurations in place, and you know how to create a quote. In this final video, we're covering Projects — how to create them, track them, and keep everything organized as your jobs move forward.

Projects are the backbone of Slabstack. Every active job lives here, and everything connects to it — your quotes, your customers, your activities, your mixes.

---

### Part 1: Creating a New Project

`[CLICK: Projects in the left-hand navigation]`
`[SHOW: Projects list view]`

Click **Projects** in the left nav. Like Quotes, this is your full list — every job, past and present.

`[CLICK: New Project button]`
`[SHOW: New project form]`

Click **New Project**.

`[ZOOM: Location dropdown]`
`[CLICK: select the appropriate location]`

Select your **location** first. Same as with quotes, this pulls in your configured defaults for buyers, other companies, and mixes.

`[ZOOM: Project Name field]`
`[TYPE: example project name — e.g., "Riverside Plaza — Foundation"]`

Give the project a clear name. Something that will make sense to you and your team at a glance.

`[ZOOM: Address and Start Date fields]`
`[TYPE: example address and date]`

Fill in the job site address and start date. These details help you filter and sort projects later, especially when you're managing multiple active jobs.

---

### Part 2: Adding Buyers, Companies, and Products

`[ZOOM: Buyer field]`
`[CLICK: select a buyer from the dropdown]`

Add your **buyer**. Your configured defaults are already here — select the customer or type to search.

`[ZOOM: Other Companies field]`

Add any **other companies** on the job — general contractor, owner, engineer. Same as you saw in quotes.

`[ZOOM: Products / Mixes section]`
`[CLICK: Add Product]`
`[CLICK: select a mix from the list]`
`[TYPE: quantity]`

Add your **products**. Select the mix, enter the quantity, and Slabstack pulls in your pricing. Add as many mixes as the job requires.

---

### Part 3: Linking a Quote

`[ZOOM: Quote field or Related Quotes section]`

If you've already created a quote for this job, you can link it directly to the project. Click the **Quote** field and search for it by name or number.

`[CLICK: select the related quote]`

Linking a quote connects your pricing to the project record, so everything stays in one place — no hunting across separate records.

---

### Part 4: Tracking Status

`[ZOOM: Project Status field or dropdown]`

Every project has a **status**. Update it as the job moves — from **Active** when work begins, to **On Hold** if something pauses it, to **Closed** when it's complete.

`[CLICK: Status dropdown]`
`[CLICK: select "Active"]`

Keeping statuses current is what makes your Projects list useful. It's how you and your team know exactly where every job stands without having to ask.

`[CLICK: Save]`
`[SHOW: Project detail page — saved and complete]`

---

### Part 5: Managing Projects in the Grid

`[CLICK: Projects in the left nav]`
`[SHOW: Projects list — multiple projects visible]`

Back in the Projects list, you'll see all your jobs in the grid. This is your command center.

`[ZOOM: filter bar and column headers]`

Use the filters to narrow by status, location, date, or customer. Sort by any column. If you're managing ten active pours this week, this view keeps everything visible and nothing slips.

---

`[SHOW: Slabstack dashboard]`

That wraps up the Slabstack onboarding series. You've got everything you need to hit the ground running — your account is configured, you can build and send quotes, log activities, and manage projects start to finish.

If you ever need help, click the chat bubble in the lower-right corner, or reach us at **success@sysdynetechnologies.com**.

Welcome to Slabstack. Let's get to work.
