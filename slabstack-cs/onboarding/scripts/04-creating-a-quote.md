# Video 4: Creating a Quote

**Series:** Slabstack Onboarding
**Runtime target:** 2:30–3:00
**Audience:** Brand new customers
**Tools:** Loom (recording) · Descript (editing) · ElevenLabs (voiceover)

---

## On-Screen Cues Legend

- `[SHOW]` — display this screen or element
- `[CLICK]` — click this item
- `[ZOOM]` — zoom or highlight this area
- `[TYPE]` — keyboard input visible on screen
- `[CUT TO]` — transition to next screen

---

## Flow

1. Navigate to Quotes in the left nav
2. Click New Quote
3. Select location — pulls configured defaults
4. Add the buyer (customer)
5. Add other companies (GC, owner, etc.) if applicable
6. Add products (mixes) with quantities — pricing auto-populates
7. Review additional line items (fuel surcharges, delivery fees, etc.)
8. Confirm terms and conditions are populated
9. Review the quote total
10. Preview the quote as a PDF
11. Send to the customer or download to share

---

## Script

---

`[SHOW: Slabstack dashboard]`

Welcome back. You've logged in, set your configurations, and you know how to log activities. Now it's time to build your first quote.

This video walks you through creating a quote from scratch — adding your customer, your mixes, your pricing, and getting it out the door.

---

### Part 1: Opening a New Quote

`[CLICK: Quotes in the left-hand navigation]`
`[SHOW: Quotes list view]`

Click **Quotes** in the left nav. This is your full quote history — every quote you've created, with status, customer, and totals at a glance.

`[CLICK: New Quote button]`
`[SHOW: New quote form]`

Click **New Quote**.

`[ZOOM: Location dropdown]`
`[CLICK: select the appropriate location]`

First, select your **location**. This is the step that pulls in everything you configured in Video 2 — your buyers, your mixes, your terms, your additional items. Choose your location and Slabstack does the rest.

---

### Part 2: Adding the Customer

`[ZOOM: Buyer field]`
`[CLICK: Buyer dropdown]`

Next, add your **buyer** — the customer you're quoting. Your configured buyers are already in the list. Click to select, or type to search.

`[CLICK: select a buyer]`

`[ZOOM: Other Companies field]`

If there are other companies on the job — a general contractor, an owner, an engineer — add them here. Same as you saw in configurations.

---

### Part 3: Adding Products and Pricing

`[ZOOM: Products / Mixes section]`
`[CLICK: Add Product or + button]`
`[SHOW: product selection dropdown or modal]`

Now add your **products**. These are your concrete mixes. Your configured mixes are already here — click to select the one you need.

`[CLICK: select a mix]`
`[ZOOM: Quantity field]`
`[TYPE: example quantity — e.g., "250"]`

Enter the quantity. Slabstack uses this to calculate the line total automatically.

`[ZOOM: Price per unit field or calculated total]`

Your unit price comes from your configured pricing. Review it, adjust if needed for this job, and move on.

`[CLICK: Add Product again if multiple mixes]`

If the job calls for multiple mixes, add each one the same way. Each line calculates independently.

---

### Part 4: Reviewing Line Items and Terms

`[ZOOM: Additional Items section]`

Below your mixes, you'll see your **additional items** — things like fuel surcharges, delivery fees, or any standard charges you set up in configurations. They're already here. Review them and remove any that don't apply to this quote.

`[ZOOM: Terms and Conditions section]`

Scroll down to **Terms and Conditions**. Your standard T&Cs are already populated from your configuration. No copying and pasting required.

`[ZOOM: Quote Total at the bottom of the form]`

At the bottom, your **quote total** updates in real time as you add or adjust items. Give it a final look before you send.

---

### Part 5: Sending the Quote

`[CLICK: Preview or Generate PDF button]`
`[SHOW: PDF preview of the quote]`

Click **Preview** to see exactly what your customer will receive. Check the layout, the numbers, and your terms.

`[CUT TO: quote form or action buttons]`
`[CLICK: Send or Email button]`

When it looks right, click **Send**. You can email it directly from Slabstack, or download the PDF and send it your own way.

`[SHOW: confirmation state or sent status on the quote record]`

The quote is logged automatically — status updated, timestamp recorded, everything tied to the customer's record.

---

`[SHOW: Quotes list view with the new quote visible]`

That's a quote in Slabstack. A few clicks, all your defaults in place, out the door.

In the next video, we'll cover Projects — how to create them, link quotes, and track jobs from start to finish.

See you there.
